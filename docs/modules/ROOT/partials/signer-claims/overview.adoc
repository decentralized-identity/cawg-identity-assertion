=== Signer claims conceptual overview

_This section is non-normative._

An *<<_identity_assertion,identity assertion>>* establishes _who_ a _<<_named_actor,named actor>>_ is by binding a _<<_verifiable_identifier,verifiable identifier>>_ to a set of _<<_c2pa_assertion,C2PA assertions>>._
It does not, by itself, describe additional validations, memberships, accreditations, or statuses that a third party may have granted to that _<<_named_actor,named actor>>._

A *<<_signer_claim,signer claim>>* provides that additional signal.
Where an *<<_identity_assertion,identity assertion>>* answers _“Who is this entity?”_, a *<<_signer_claim,signer claim>>* answers _“What additional validation, status, or accreditation does this entity have?”_

Examples of the kinds of statements a *<<_signer_claim,signer claim>>* can carry include:

* membership of an industry body (for example, a verified news publisher program)
* a licensing or accreditation status granted by a regulator
* membership of, or a relationship with, a wire service or agency

These statements are made by a *<<_signer_claim_authority,signer claim authority>>* — an entity that is distinct from the issuer of the _<<_named_actor,named actor’s>>_ own _<<_verifiable_identifier,verifiable identifier>>_ and from any certificate authority involved in that identifier’s lifecycle.
A *<<_signer_claim,signer claim>>* is therefore decoupled from certificate issuance and from certificate lifecycle events such as renewal or revocation.

The mechanism described in this section is designed around the following principles:

. *Separation of concerns.* Establishing identity and attesting to additional status are handled by independent mechanisms and, typically, independent entities.
. *Offline validation.* A validator can verify a *<<_signer_claim,signer claim>>* without contacting the issuing *<<_signer_claim_authority,authority>>* or any other network service.
. *Historical validation.* By reusing the existing trusted time stamp mechanism, a *<<_signer_claim,signer claim>>* can be validated long after it was issued, even after the *<<_signer_claim_authority,authority’s>>* keys have rotated.
. *No per-ecosystem code.* A validator that understands this mechanism does not need new code when a new *<<_signer_claim_authority,authority>>* or a new kind of claim is introduced. Adding support for a new ecosystem is a matter of configuration and, optionally, metadata — not a software update.
. *Compact representation.* A *<<_signer_claim,signer claim>>* carries neither certificate chains nor trust anchors. Trust is established out of band through a *<<_signer_claim_authority_registry,signer claim authority registry>>.*

[IMPORTANT]
.Trust is not implied by inclusion
====
_This section is non-normative._

The presence of a *<<_signer_claim,signer claim>>* in a _<<C2PA Manifest>>_ does not, by itself, mean that its statement should be surfaced to an end user.
A statement should be surfaced only when the *<<_signer_claim_authority,authority>>* that issued it appears in the validator’s *<<_signer_claim_authority_registry,signer claim authority registry>>* and the claim passes all validation steps described in xref:_validating_a_signer_claim[xrefstyle=full].
