==== Verifiable credential binding conceptual overview

_This section is non-normative._

https://www.w3.org/TR/vc-data-model-2.0/[W3C Verifiable Credentials] (VCs) and https://www.w3.org/TR/did-1.0/[Decentralized Identifiers] (DIDs) provide a standardized mechanism for expressing identity-related statements about a _<<named actor>>_ in a way that is portable, issuer-independent, and cryptographically verifiable.
When used in conjunction with an *<<_identity_assertion,identity assertion,>>* these technologies enable a _<<_named_actor,named actor>>_ to associate verifiable identity information with creative work without relying on a single platform, service, or registry.

A Verifiable Credential represents a statement made by an issuer about a subject identified by a DID.
In the context of an *<<_identity_assertion,identity assertion,>>* such credentials may attest to attributes relevant to authorship or contribution, such as roles, affiliations, professional recognition, or the use of a particular name or pseudonym.
These credentials are held by the _<<_named_actor,named actor>>_ and may be selectively presented as supporting evidence when making an *<<_identity_assertion,identity assertion.>>*

Decentralized Identifiers provide a stable identifier for a _<<_named_actor,named actor>>_ that is not inherently bound to a specific platform or account.
While the cryptographic material and associated services linked to a DID may change over time, the identifier itself can remain consistent.
This property supports durable attribution and verifiable credit for creative works across platforms and over long periods of time.

Unlike _<<_identity_claims_aggregation,identity claims aggregation>>_ credentials, which combine multiple claims into a single issued artifact, Verifiable Credentials are presented directly by the _<<_named_actor,named actor.>>_
This presentation model allows the _<<_named_actor,named actor>>_ to select which credentials to disclose, and in what context, when asserting identity in relation to a work.
Multiple independent issuers may provide credentials about the same _<<_named_actor,named actor,>>_ enabling an *<<_identity_assertion,identity assertion>>* to be supported by attestations from diverse sources without requiring prior coordination among issuers.

Credential ecosystems such as those developed by the https://www.firstperson.network[First Person Project] illustrate how first-person, holder-mediated credentials can support self-authored identity in open creative systems.
*<<_identity_assertion,Identity assertions>>* may incorporate such credentials alongside other verifiable attestations, enabling flexible and interoperable expressions of identity that align with the goals of creator agency and portability.

By integrating W3C Verifiable Credentials and DIDs, this specification enables identity assertions to reference verifiable identity information that is controlled by the _<<_named_actor,named actor>>_ and verifiable by relying parties using open standards.
This approach supports decentralized attribution while allowing evaluators of *<<_identity_assertion,identity assertions>>* to apply their own trust frameworks and policies when assessing presented credentials.
